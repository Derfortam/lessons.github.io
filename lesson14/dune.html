ATREIDES

"We are house Atreides. There is no call we do not answer. There is no faith that we betray"

The House of Atrid is a noble family of great plot importance in the Dune universe created by writer Frank Herbert. House Atrid is one of the Great Houses of the intergalactic feudal empire. Representatives of the House of Atrid play a significant role in each novel of the series. The author traces this dynasty back to the famous ancient Greek family of Atrides, which suffered a tragic fate for several generations (Atreus, Thyestes, Agamemnon, Orestes, etc.). Since the time of the Butlerian Jihad, the Atrides' homeworld has been the planet Kaladan. The first representatives of the family to permanently reside on the planet were Estes and Cagin, the sons of Veronica Tergiet and Vorian Atrid, the son of the Titan Agamemnon. Under Emperor Shaddam IV, the Atrides gained possession of the desert planet Arrakis, and the family moved to Arrakin, its capital.

SARDAUKAR

"Hope overshadows observation" - Venerable Mother Moghiyam

The Sardaukars or Imperial Sardaukars are soldiers of the elite guard of the Padishah Emperors of Corrino from the Dune universe. The prototype of the Padishah Empire in the Dune books is usually the Ottoman Empire. Accordingly, the Sardaukars are compared to the Turkish infantry, the Janissaries.

The Sardaukar were fanatically loyal to the Padishah-Emperors of the House of Corrino, the Great House that ruled the entire known universe before and during the events described in Dune. Before Paul Atrid organised the Fremen on Arrakis, the Sardaukar were the most powerful military organisation in the Empire, and the most feared. The Sardaukars were the force that kept the House of Corrino on the throne, so in the course of their service, Sardaukars could achieve high social status. From an early age, the Sardaukars trained on the planet Salus Secundus, the ancestral planet of the House of Corrino, which became the "prison planet" of the Corrino Emperors after the atomic bombing. The Sardaukars were organised into legions, each consisting of 10 brigades, with a total of 30,000 soldiers in a legion. Before the events on Arrakis, one legion could restore order on any planet that dared to provoke the Emperor's anger.

HARKONNEN

"Instruments of power should always be at hand... power and fear should be sharp and ready"

The House of Harkonnen is a powerful family that is of great plot importance in the Dune universe created by author Frank Herbert. House Harkonnen is one of the Great Houses of the galactic feudal empire. The House of Harkonnen plays a significant role in the Dune Universe. According to the House of Harkonnen book, their coat of arms is a blue griffin, but in the Dune Encyclopedia, the coat of arms is a ram's head

The Harkonnens are the main opponents of the Atrid family and are described as their antipodes. Their enmity began with a misunderstanding between the ancestors of their families in the last years of the war between humans and sentient machines. While the Atrides are described as noble men of honour, the Harkonnens are portrayed as ambitious, not above meanness and intrigue.

FREEMAN

"Sophistication comes from cities and wisdom from the desert"

The Fremen are a tribe of people in the Dune universe, a series of books by American writer Frank Herbert. The Fremen live on the planet Arrakis, also known as Dune. also known as Dune, a desert planet that is the only source of spice from which they make melange. The Freemen arrived on Dune thousands of years before the events of the novel as Zen Sunni travellers, a religious sect (its name appears to be a hybrid of Zen Buddhism and Sunnism) in exile. Over the centuries of living on Arrakis, the Freemen have adapted to its harsh climate. They called themselves the Free People of the Dune.

Freemen live in sitcoms. Everyone has their own nayb, whose word is law until someone rejects their leadership in a fight. Polygamy is practiced. The Freeman system of justice is based on court fights. The naib of the tribe is the person who killed the previous naib in a duel. Any Freeman can challenge another to a duel under any pretext related to a violation of the rules of etiquette, law, etc.; the winner assumes responsibility for the wife, children and part of the property of the loser and, accordingly, satisfies his demands that led to the duel. Duels are held without distichcomb, so that by specially processing the body of the loser into water, the losses during the fight are reimbursed to the winner.
